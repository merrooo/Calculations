🔷 1. Positive Sequence Impedance Z₁ (Ω/km)

📐 Calculator

Result will appear here

📖 Discussion

Positive sequence impedance is the impedance seen by balanced three-phase currents. It represents the line's inherent opposition to normal load and symmetrical faults. The reactance \(X_1\) depends on conductor geometry: larger phase spacing increases reactance; larger conductor radius (GMR) decreases it.

Physical meaning: \(X_1 = 0.06283 \ln(D_{eq}/GMR)\) Ω/km at 50 Hz. This formula derives from Ampere's law and flux linkage.

📐 Equations:
\( Z_1 = R_{ac} + j X_1 \)   where   \( X_1 = 0.06283 \cdot \ln\left(\dfrac{D_{eq}}{GMR}\right) \) Ω/km (50 Hz)
\( D_{eq} = \sqrt[3]{D_{ab} D_{bc} D_{ca}} \) (geometric mean distance)

—

📖 Discussion

Zero sequence impedance is the impedance seen by zero sequence currents during ground faults. It includes the earth return path and is typically 2.5–3.5 times larger than \(Z_1\). Overhead shield wires (ground wires) increase \(X_0\) significantly (k_sw = 2.5–3.0).

Earth return resistance \(R_e = 9.88\times10^{-4}f\) Ω/km and depth \(D_e = 658.5\sqrt{\rho/f}\) m depend on soil resistivity.

📐 Equations:
\( Z_0 = (R_{ac} + 3R_e) + j \cdot k_{sw} \cdot X_{0,base} \)
\( R_e = 9.88\times10^{-4} f \)   \( D_e = 658.5\sqrt{\rho/f} \)   \( X_{0,base} = 0.06283 \ln(D_e / GMR_0) \)

🔁 3. Zero-Sequence Compensation Factor k₀

📐 Calculator

—

📖 Discussion

k₀ adjusts ground fault measurements for the difference between \(Z_0\) and \(Z_1\). For single line-to-ground faults, the relay uses compensated current \(I_A + k_0 I_{res}\). Typical 220 kV: \(k_0 \approx 0.67\angle -3^\circ\), so \(1+k_0 \approx 1.67\), meaning fault resistance is magnified ~1.67×.

📐 Equation: \( k_0 = \dfrac{Z_0 - Z_1}{3 Z_1} \) (complex)

📖 Discussion

Mho relay operates if \( |Z_{meas} - Z_{reach}| \le |Z_{reach}| \). The circle passes through origin → inherently directional. Most common distance relay characteristic.

Equation: \( |Z_{meas} - Z_{reach}| \le |Z_{reach}| \) → Trip

📖 Discussion

Earth return resistance \(R_e\) and equivalent depth \(D_e\) are essential for zero sequence impedance. Soil resistivity \(\rho\) varies from 10 Ω·m (wet soil) to 1000+ Ω·m (rock).
